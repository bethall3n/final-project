Visit Thailand


Gleaming temples and golden Buddhas frame the landscapes in Thailand, serving as a stunning backdrop for tours of Bangkok noodle shacks and adventures along the luxurious coastline. Thailand is an incredible country that boasts glistening waters scattered with traditional wooden long boats and pristine beaches. It is a wonderful place to explore and is loved by many.



Must see activities

Thailand has an abundance of activties and excursions to suit everyone! Whether you want to island hop, walk with the elephants or visit some of the beautiful temples the country has to offer, you will never be short of ideas. We have added some of top attractions that you do not want to miss on your next trip to Thailand...


Wat Arun
Bangkok

Wat Arun is a temple that rises from the river's banks. Today the temple is one of Bangkok's most iconic structures – not to mention one of the few Buddhist temples you are encouraged to climb on.

The Grand Palace
Bangkok

The Grand Palace is a former royal residence that was consecrated in 1782. Today, it remains the city's biggest tourist attraction and a pilgrimage destination for Buddhists.

Elephant Nature Park
Chiang Mai

A leading elephant rescue and rehabilitation center. Today, Elephant Nature Park is home to over 100 elephants who live free from abuse within family herds

Railay Beach
Krabi

Railay, also known as Rai Leh, is a small peninsula between the city of Krabi and Ao Nang in Thailand. It is accessible only by boat due to high limestone cliffs cutting off mainland access.

Khao Sok National Park
Khao Sok

Home to some of the world’s oldest rainforests, Khao Sok National Park is a stunning tropical jungle in southern Thailand. It is easily one of the most stunning national parks in Thailand.

The White Temple
Chiang Rai

Wat Rong Khun, better known as the White Temple, is a Buddhist temple and one of the most recognizable temples in Pa O Don Chai, Mueang District, Chiang Rai Province, Thailand.

Frequently Asked Questions

The best time to visit depends on what you want to experience! Thailand has 3 distinct seasons which we have summarised for you below:

  • Dry/Cool Season: Between November & February
  • Hot Season: Between March & May
  • Wet Season: Between May & October

    • All travellers to Thailand must carry a passport with a validity period of 6 months or more and proof of onward passage (a return or through ticket). You may be refused entry to Thailand if your passport is damaged or has pages missing. British and American passport holders arriving by air or land can enter Thailand for 60 days without a visa, this can be extended once for up to 30 days. You must apply for an extension before your visa exception period ends.

    We recommend lightweight cotton clothing to be comfortable in Thailand’s generally tropical temperatures, but pack warm layers if you’re visiting northern Thailand between Nov – Feb. Bring a couple of cover-ups to protect against mosquito bites when outside in the evening, and a lightweight raincoat is a good idea if visiting during the rainy season as the downpours can be very heavy.
